I think too much of our lens has been dominated by imperative algorithmic thinking, and not thinking principally about the paradigm that we are in. We need to consider every aspect of our design through the lens of structural analysis on omni-directional cells and omni-directional data-flows.

If you're thinking "this algorithm says... "  that's a process smell in propagators.

If you say, on the other hand, "This fixpoint computation over monotonic data flow guarantees... " (or similar), you know you're in the right design territory.

The data flow IS the algorithm. The data flow is the OPTIMAL expression of solving the computation.


Strong Postulate: Any computation can be expressed as a fixpoint over some interconnected lattice structures (our propagator networks).

Corollary: For any given computation, along some axis of "optimality", there is an optimal computation on such structure.


Now, I think this Postulate and it Corollary, we can poke at, strengthen, and refine. But I think the gist of it should be our guiding consideration when building on propagators. We can call this the Hyperlattice Conjecture (Or the Propagator Network Conjecture).


I would like for us to audit our current design through this lens, and consider if we are fulfilling it. This is what it means to be Propagator First. We should really change that principle to "Propagator Only".

I feel that the design for this has stepped back from this number one, core principle; and I think we can do better.
