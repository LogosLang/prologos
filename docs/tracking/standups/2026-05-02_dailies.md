## Lessons surfaced this session (worth codifying)

### 1. Master-roadmap-vs-series-masters drift discipline

**Observation**: The series masters were ALL more current than the master
roadmap. PPN Master, SRE Master, BSP-LE Master correctly tracked completed
tracks; the roadmap's per-series status lines and tracks tables were stale.

**Hypothesis**: Roadmap drift happens because PIRs trigger updates to the
series master (which is the canonical view) but the corresponding roadmap row
is sometimes missed. The series master IS the source of truth; the roadmap is
a navigation index that drifts unless explicitly synced.

**Discipline candidate** (worth codifying in `workflow.md` if pattern recurs):
when a track closes (PIR), update the series master AND the corresponding
roadmap row in the SAME commit. Or add to the post-close checklist: "verify
roadmap row matches master-tracker row."

**Promotion gate**: One more occurrence of roadmap drift on a track close →
codify as workflow.md discipline.

### 2. Adversarial framing as refinement, not destruction

**Observation**: User explicitly framed the adversarial round on PReduce's
three-layer decomposition as "Yes, and" — refining/validating rather than
tearing down. Result: six refinements emerged (per-AST-PU compound regions
NOT per-node PUs; one rewriting + one retraction stratum NOT six strata;
unified rule registry NOT two registries; e-class poset NOT just semilattice;
opaque-evaluation boundary; retraction-bit consultation regime). Framework
held; got tighter.

**Hypothesis**: Adversarial rounds work best when applied to a model the
designer trusts well enough to defend at every challenge. "Yes, and" is the
right disposition for tightening; "tear down" only fits when the model is
suspect. The methodology rule (`workflow.md`) says adversarial framing must
surface drift — it doesn't say the model must break.

**Promotion gate**: One more "Yes, and" adversarial round that tightens
without breaking → codify as a recognized adversarial-round disposition in
`workflow.md` / `CRITIQUE_METHODOLOGY.org`.

### 3. Series-promotion artifact pattern (master + roadmap-update commits)

**Observation**: PReduce founding pattern (today) was: master doc creation
+ roadmap section addition + same-commit register. SH founding pattern
(2026-04-30) was: master + path note + super-optimization research note,
followed by roadmap update later. The master-and-roadmap-update pattern
gives faster discoverability; the master+companion-notes pattern gives
more depth for series with substantial foundational research.

**Hypothesis**: For series with extensive prior research (PReduce had 5+
research notes already), the master + roadmap update is sufficient at
founding. For series founded from a single research arc (SH had ongoing
research conversation), the three-artifact pattern is appropriate.

**Promotion gate**: Look for the pattern in next 1-2 series foundings →
codify in `DESIGN_METHODOLOGY.org` Stage 1 as recognized series-opening
shapes.
