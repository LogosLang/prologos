### Design Decisions Made

1. **Parse tree IS the representation** — no datum layer, no compat layer, no syntax objects. SRE `ctor-desc` on `parse-tree-node` directly.
2. **CALM-compliant pipeline** — set-once cells between strata. Rewrites at stratum boundaries (Layered Recovery). No replacement merge.
3. **Tag refinement as SRE subtype** — T(0) stratum assigns form-head tags via first-token inspection. Approach 3 (field) with Approach 4 awareness (cell).
4. **V(0) sub-strata** — V(0,0) implicit-map → V(0,1) dot-access → V(0,2) infix. Data dependency enforced structurally, not by priority.
5. **9 simple + 5 recursive + 4 specialized** rule classification.
6. **Provenance for source ordering** — errors carry structural conflict information from the provenance chain.

### Lessons

- **The Pocket Universe Principle applied to parsing**: a `parse-tree-node` with RRB children IS a tree. A PVec of PVecs. Structural sharing makes rewriting efficient. The SRE decomposes into the embedded lattice — one mechanism for type decomposition AND surface normalization.
- **CALM violations hide in priority ordering**: when rules have a DATA dependency, priority ordering within one BSP round is imperative sequencing in disguise. The BSP scheduler as default would catch this as wrong results. Sub-strata are the correct-by-construction fix.
- **NTT modeling as design validation**: expressing the architecture in NTT syntax revealed 3 gaps (rewrite, refine, foreach) that the prose design missed. Every propagator track should include an NTT modeling section.
- **External critique found the recursive-rule gap**: 5 of the "14 simple rules" actually need fold-over-children. The design was over-simplifying to maintain the "all rules are data" narrative. Honest classification (9 simple + 5 recursive + 4 specialized) is more actionable.
