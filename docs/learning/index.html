Why these texts

Prologos's design rests on a thesis that every computable function is expressible as a fixpoint computation on lattices (the Hyperlattice Conjecture). The compiler runs as a propagator network where every cell carries a lattice value, every merge is a lattice join, and every typing rule is a structural unification — the lattice meet. The math foundations matter directly to the engineering.

These three texts cover the foundations from complementary angles: Călugăreanu for the module-theoretic perspective (lattices as algebraic structures over modules), Freese-Ježek-Nation for the free-lattice / word-problem / decidability perspective, and Nation's notes for the broad lattice-theory survey. Read together, they're the math equivalent of a triangulation: the same structures, viewed from three angles, the points of agreement are where Prologos's design lives.
